Town
Annemarie Ní Churreáin & Rich Gilligan

about town

The original print editions of Town were designed, type-set and letterpress printed by Jamie Murphy at the Distiller's Press at the National College of Art & Design (NCAD) in Dublin, and then published under Murphy's imprint, The Salvage Press. There were two print editions, Deluxe and Standard. Town was published in 2018.

The typeface in both editions is Adrian Frutiger’s Méridien, first released by Deberny & Peignot in 1957. The books use water-marked 200 gsm handmade paper which was commissioned especially from the Velké Losiny Mill in the Czech Republic. The bindings were executed by Craig Jensen, with the assistance of Marc Hammonds, at BookLab II, San Marcos, Texas.

Town was printed in a run of 80 copies.

54 copies of the Standard edition were printed. The Standard edition copies are bound in painted cloth over boards, housed in a cloth slipcase (390 x 285 x 20 mm). Copies marked i–iv were gifted to collaborators, while the remainder are numbered 1-50.

Copies of the Deluxe edition are lettered A-Z. These editions are bound in full alum tawed goat and are each housed, accompanied by a portfolio containing eight additional photographic prints, in a painted cloth covered solandar box (390 x 285 x 35 mm).

Town features photography by Rich Gilligan. The photographs were captured in Dublin city over the last days of 2017. They were enlarged by hand from the 35mm negatives onto resin coated Ilford MGIV by Barbara Wilson at her darkroom in London.

collaborators

ANNEMARIE NÍ CHURREEÁIN is a poet from North West Donegal. She has been awarded literary fellowships by Akademie Schloss Solitude (Germany), Jack Kerouac House (Florida) and Hawthornden Castle (Scotland). Her work has been published in Poetry Ireland Review, The SHOp, The London Magazine, Agenda Poetry Journal and The Stinging Fly. In 2016, Annemarie was the recipient of a Next Generation Artists Award from the Arts Council of Ireland. In 2017, Annemarie was appointed to the Writers In Prisons Panel co-funded by the Arts Council & the Department of Justice, Equality and Reform. She has published two collections of poetry, Bloodroot (Doire 2017) and The Poison Glen (The Gallery Press 2021).

RICH GILLIGAN is known for his distinctive approach to fashion and portraiture, with a style that exists somewhere between classic reportage and fine art photography. Rich's photography has been widely exhibited, with recent shows in Dali, Malmo, Dublin, London, Munich, Los Angeles, New York, Paris, Sydney and Copenhagen. He was awarded the 2013 Showcase Photography Award from the Gallery of Photography, Dublin for emerging talent in Europe and was recently selected by Time Magazine as one of the top nine photographers to watch. Rich is currently based in upstate New York.

JAMIE MURPHY is an artist, designer, and publisher. He founded The Salvage Press. Educated at the National College of Art & Design (NCAD), his career in publishing began in the UK at Justin Knopp's newly established letterpress workshop, Typoretum. Returning to Ireland he worked as an understudy to master printer Seán Sills at Distillers Press in NCAD. He began as Designer-in-Residence in 2013 before being appointed Printer in 2015. His focus with students is on typographic research and experimentation. Through collaboration, The Salvage Press works with creatives from many disciplines producing projects which are mainly of Irish interest. Jamie's award winning books reside in private, public and institutional collections across the globe.
